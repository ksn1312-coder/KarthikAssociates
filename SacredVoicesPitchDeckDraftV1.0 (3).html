The Problem

India's faith heritage is the world's richest — and the least explained

🏛️

5 lakh+ faith sites,
no audio guides

Temples, churches, mosques, gurudwaras, viharas — millions of visitors arrive with no contextual information on history, ritual, or architecture. India's greatest heritage remains unnarrated.

🌐

65% prefer content
in their native tongue

A Tamil Catholic at Velankanni, a Punjabi Muslim at Ajmer Sharif, a Bengali at a Kolkata church — none receive contextual audio in their own language. Language is the missing bridge.

💸

Guides are costly
or non-existent

Professional guides charge ₹500–2,000 per visit. Audio kiosks are rare, English-only, and unreliable. Rural and Tier-2 sites have nothing at all. The Louvre serves 60 languages.
