Conflicts & Gaps

What Needs to Be Fixed

Direct contradictions and missing information across both documents.

Direct Conflicts

Conflict
Generator Specification Mismatch
The Franchise Proposal specifies a 30 KVA generator (₹5.64L) to run 10 deep freezers 24/7 for B2B cold chain. The Pitch Deck calls for a 7.5–10 KVA unit for outlet + gaming + cold storage. These describe the same physical location with fundamentally different power infrastructure — one cannot be right without making the other wrong.
Action: Decide the final spec. B2B hub = 30 KVA (₹5.64L). Outlet-first = 10 KVA (~₹1.5L). If phased, show Phase 1 and Phase 2 capex separately in the Excel and both documents.
Conflict
Multi-Concept vs Single-Brand Outlet
The Proposal describes a pure BonBon ice cream parlour with a back-room stock point. The Pitch Deck describes HappyZone — a 5-concept lifestyle venue where BonBon is one of five revenue streams. These are materially different setups with different floor plans, licensing, staff skills, and brand arrangements.
Action: Confirm whether BonBon Coimbatore has sanctioned the multi-concept layout. The franchise agreement may restrict branding or space usage. Add a clarification note or slide to the Deck.

Information Gaps

Gap
Franchise Fee Not Disclosed
Neither document states the one-time franchise fee payable to BonBon Coimbatore. This is a material capex line item — every investor and banker will ask for it.
Action: Obtain from the franchise agreement. Add to the Excel Capex sheet and the Pitch Deck investment slide. Include in the ₹25.96L total or as a separate line.
Gap
No Launch Marketing Budget
Both documents are silent on what it costs to acquire the first walk-in customers — Swiggy/Zomato onboarding, opening promotions, social media setup, apartment flyers. Walk-in ramp depends on awareness.
Action: Add ₹50K–₹75K as a one-time launch budget. Include in capex (Excel) and in the OpEx section of the Proposal and Operations slide of the Deck.
Gap — Deck
Competitor Analysis Missing from Pitch Deck
The detailed Google Maps competitor survey (ibaco 5 km away, no Polar Bear or Giani in the micro-market, Baskin Robbins at premium pricing) exists in the Proposal but not in the Deck. It is your strongest defensible market claim.
Action: Add 4–5 key data points to Slide 2 (Market Opportunity). "Zero dedicated ice cream parlour in Pudupakkam" is a single line that investors will remember.
Gap — Deck
7-Zone Rollout Has No Unit Economics
The Deck promises 7-zone coverage but doesn't show how each zone's economics stack. The Proposal shows the Pudupakkam model clearly. The Deck should summarise it.
Action: Add a 'Zone Economics Summary' table to the Revenue Model slide: avg units/zone, revenue/zone, contribution margin. The Excel has this data — surface it in the deck.
Gap — Both
Working Capital Buffer Understated
The Proposal allocates ₹1L working capital — enough for ~5 weeks during a 4-month loss period of ₹88K/month. This is a 3.4-month shortfall that could stall the business before it reaches breakeven.
Action: Increase working capital provision to ₹2.5–3L. Update the Proposal capex table, Excel Capex sheet. Total investment rises to ~₹27.5–28.5L.

Recommended Actions

What to Fix — In Order

Eight specific steps to reconcile, strengthen, and align both documents before investor outreach.

01
Reconcile Fix the Generator Specification
Decide before any investor meeting. B2B hub = 30 KVA (₹5.64L). Outlet-first = 10 KVA (~₹1.5L). If phased, show Phase 1 and Phase 2 capex separately. Update both the Proposal's capex table and the Pitch Deck's Operations slide and Excel Capex sheet.
02
Reconcile Clarify Breakeven vs Payback Language
Change the Pitch Deck from "6–9 months breakeven" to "6–9 months to operating breakeven (monthly OpEx covered); 20–28 months for full capex recovery." This is honest, consistent with the Proposal's projections, and still compelling.
03
Add to Deck Add a "Phase 1 vs Full Vision" Slide
Investors need to understand that Phase 1 is the Pudupakkam mini outlet (₹25.96L, proven Proposal model) and Phase 2 is the 7-zone Master Franchise rollout. A single slide showing this progression removes all confusion between the two documents.
04
Add to Deck Add Competitor Gap Evidence to Slide 2
The Google Maps survey (zero ice cream parlour in Pudupakkam, ibaco 5 km away, no Polar Bear or Giani in the corridor) is your strongest defensible market claim. It exists in the Proposal — add 4–5 key data points to the Market Opportunity slide in the Deck.
05
Update Add Franchise Fee to Investment Table
Obtain the one-time BonBon franchise fee from the agreement with BonBon Coimbatore. Add it to the ₹25.96L total in the Proposal's capex summary and Excel Capex sheet. Presenting an incomplete investment figure to investors is a credibility risk.
06
Update Increase Working Capital Provision
Raise working capital from ₹1L to ₹2.5–3L. Conservative scenario shows ₹88K/mo loss for months 1–4. Three months of buffer = ₹2.64L minimum. Update the Proposal capex table and Excel. Total investment rises to ~₹27.5–28.5L.
07
Add to Deck Add Scoop-Level Margin Proof to Revenue Slide
Add 2–3 lines to the Revenue Model slide: ₹325/tub → 35 scoops → ₹84/scoop → 10–17% raw material cost → 40% gross margin. Investors who ask "how do you make money on ice cream?" deserve a concrete, memorable answer. The numbers are already in the Proposal.
08
Add to Both Add Launch Marketing Budget Line
Add ₹50K–₹75K as a one-time launch budget covering: Swiggy/Zomato onboarding support, opening week offers, apartment complex flyers, Instagram/GMB setup. Add to capex in the Excel and the OpEx section of the Proposal and Operations slide of the Deck.
